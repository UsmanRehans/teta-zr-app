## 2. Health: Home-Cooked vs Restaurant Food

### Key Findings

- Home-cooked meals have **significantly higher nutritional quality** with lower sodium, fat, and added sugars compared to commercially prepared foods.
- Children eating home-cooked meals have a **significantly higher probability** of consuming fruits and vegetables.
- Americans who eat home-cooked meals **every day are more likely to describe their diets as extremely or very healthy**.
- Nearly **90% of Americans** eat home-cooked meals at least a few times a week, vs only **17%** ordering takeout frequently.

### Comparison Table (for the page)

| Dimension | Home-Cooked | Fast Food / Chains |
|-----------|-------------|-------------------|
| Sodium & fat | Lower | High |
| Fruit & vegetable content | Higher | Low |
| Preservatives | None (fresh ingredients) | Yes (shelf-life driven) |
| Portions | Made with care | Oversized for profit |
| Personal touch | Every dish is unique | Standardized recipes |
| Community money | Stays local | Leaves immediately |

### Sources

- [NIH/PMC — Home-Cooked Meals & Nutritious Ingredients](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC6487205/)

## 4. Local Multiplier Effect

| Scenario | Amount staying local | Source |
|----------|---------------------|--------|
| $100 spent at **local independents** | **$45** | [iLocal Inc](https://ilocalinc.org/why-shop-local/the-local-multiplier-effect-latest-research/) |
| $100 spent at a **big-box chain** | **$14** | [iLocal Inc](https://ilocalinc.org/why-shop-local/the-local-multiplier-effect-latest-research/) |

- Local businesses recirculate money **2–3× more** within the community.
- Restaurants/service providers generate larger multipliers because they're **labor-intensive** — more revenue goes to local payroll.

### Sources

- [New Economics Foundation — Local Multiplier Effect](https://amiba.net/local-multiplier/)
- [GEO — Why Local Multiplier Always Counts](https://geo.coop/articles/why-local-multiplier-effect-always-counts)

## 7. Cultural Significance of "Teta's Cooking"

### Key Quotes & Context

- **"Teta"** = grandmother in Lebanese Arabic. Deeply respected family role.
- Sunday lunch with family is a **practically sacred tradition** in Lebanon.
- Grandmothers are described as "strong, resourceful women with invaluable cooking wisdom."

> "When we lose our traditional kitchens, we lose more than recipes. We lose time carved out to cook and connect. We lose the smell of home, the sight of our mothers' and grandmothers' hands, the laughter in the kitchen."
>
> — [The Public Source](https://thepublicsource.org/mouneh-zero-waste-cooking)

- The transfer of culinary knowledge across generations is a hallmark of Lebanese culture. Grandmothers serve as **custodians of tradition**.
